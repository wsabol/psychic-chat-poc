# Tarot Card Correspondences - Major Arcana

This reference guide provides traditional correspondences for the Major Arcana based primarily on the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn system, which is the most widely recognized framework in Western esoteric traditions.

## Notes on the Correspondences

**Hebrew Letters:** The 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet correspond to the 22 Major Arcana cards. Each letter has a literal meaning and a numerical value (gematria).

**Astrology:** The Golden Dawn system assigns planetary, zodiacal, or elemental correspondences to each card. The three "mother" letters (Aleph, Mem, Shin) correspond to the three elements (Air, Water, Fire).

**Colors:** Based on the Golden Dawn "King Scale" color correspondences. Different color scales exist for various purposes.

**Musical Notes:** Paul Foster Case developed musical correspondences based on the Hebrew letters and their astrological associations. Some variation exists between different systems.

**Herbs & Essential Oils:** Derived from planetary and zodiacal associations. Multiple herbs/oils may correspond to each card based on their traditional magical and medicinal properties.

**Important:** Different authors and traditions may assign these correspondences differently. The book you mentioned likely has its own unique system. This represents the most commonly used Golden Dawn-based system, which influenced the Rider-Waite-Smith deck and many modern tarot traditions.
